What does this site do?

This site provides an animated visualization of the station location of JT65 / JT9 signals transmitted and received by Amateur Radio operators over time. I built this site to look at propagation patterns change, to visually see the location of stations I have received as an approximate indication of how propagation paths change over time.

How accurate is this for predicting propagation conditions?

The display of received stations can only display stations based on data you or other operators upload, and the stations received during the time period that you or the other stations were actively receiving.

For best results therefore, data received over a longer period of time (multiple hours) is going to be more interesting to visualize that data received over a relatively short period of time like 15 minutes.

This visualization is also completely dependent on remote stations transmitting over a period of time. For example, if you receive station A transmitting over a 10 minute period but then their tranmissions stop, this doesn't necessarily mean that the propagation path to that station has closed, because they may have just turned off their radio. Even still, receiving stations in a general location is a positive indication that a path is (or was) open to that location, and so is still interesting to see visually on a map over time how this may change.
